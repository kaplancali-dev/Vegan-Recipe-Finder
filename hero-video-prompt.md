# HARVEST — Hero Video Prompt (8 seconds)

## Scene: Kitchen Discovery

**Setting:** A real, lived-in kitchen — modern but not magazine-perfect. Golden-hour sunlight through large windows. A wooden cutting board sits on the counter, maybe a single lemon beside it. A small herb pot on the windowsill. The kitchen looks used, not staged.

**Camera:** Medium shot, slightly wider than portrait — we see the couple from about waist-up, with enough of the kitchen visible (counter, window, sink) to feel the space. The fridge is open to one side, slightly out of focus.

**Fridge (VERY IMPORTANT — must look like a real person's fridge):**
- DOOR SHELVES: Only bottles and condiments — a bottle of soy sauce, a jar of mustard, a bottle of apple cider vinegar, a carton of oat milk. NO produce on the door shelves ever. Real fridge doors hold bottles, jars, and cartons only.
- MAIN INTERIOR SHELVES: Mostly empty. One bag of spinach, one bell pepper, one avocado, a container of leftover food with a lid. That's it. Lots of visible empty shelf space. This fridge belongs to someone who needs to go shopping soon — that's the whole point of the scene.
- NO kale bushes, NO piles of tomatoes, NO duplicate items. A real midweek fridge is sparse.

**Counter (VERY IMPORTANT — must be mostly empty):**
- A wooden cutting board with a knife on it
- A phone lying flat on the counter
- One block of tofu (still in packaging)
- One lemon
- That is ALL. The rest of the counter is clear, clean wood or stone surface. No produce piles, no bowls of fruit, no grocery bags. Two people figuring out what to make for dinner do not have 12 vegetables spread in front of them.

**Characters:** A young East Asian woman with dark hair pulled up in a casual bun, wearing a white linen shirt under a natural-toned canvas apron — she looks like she's mid-cooking, comfortable in this kitchen. Beside her, a tall man with light brown hair and a short beard, wearing a soft chambray denim button-down, sleeves loosely rolled. They have easy, familiar chemistry — a real couple, not models. Their body language is relaxed and close.

**Action (8-second arc):**

- **0–3s:** They're standing near the open fridge, close together, sharing a quiet moment looking at a phone one of them is holding. Relaxed, natural posture. No exaggerated expressions — just two people figuring out dinner.
- **3–5s:** Something on the phone makes them both smile, then laugh — genuine, unforced. The kind of laugh where she leans into him slightly. It builds naturally, not all at once.
- **5–7s:** Still smiling, she glances toward the fridge. He follows her look. A small moment of "oh wait, we actually can." No big gestures — just a shared realization.
- **7–8s:** They're turning together toward the counter, the energy shifting from "what do we eat" to "let's do this." The scene ends mid-motion, mid-laugh — not posed.

**Mood:** This should feel like a home video someone's friend accidentally captured. Intimate, warm, effortless. The couple is the story — the food is just context. Think "Sunday evening, no plans, figuring it out together."

**Lighting:** Natural golden-hour warmth from the windows, backlighting the couple softly. No studio lighting. Shadows are gentle, not dramatic.

**Color palette:** Warm wood tones, soft golden light, muted natural greens. Nothing oversaturated.

**Audio:** None. Generate video only — no audio track whatsoever. Music will be added separately in post-production.

**Critical — what to AVOID:**
- NO MORE THAN 4 TOTAL FOOD ITEMS ON THE COUNTER (cutting board, phone, tofu block, lemon — that's the complete list)
- NO MORE THAN 4 TOTAL FOOD ITEMS INSIDE THE FRIDGE (bag of spinach, one bell pepper, one avocado, one container)
- FRIDGE DOOR SHELVES: ONLY bottles/jars/cartons — NEVER produce, NEVER greens, NEVER vegetables
- NO duplicate items anywhere (not two avocados, not two lemons, not two of anything)
- NO kale — it photographs too dramatically and overwhelms the frame
- NO bowls of fruit anywhere in the scene
- NO bags of groceries, NO grocery-store-style arrangements
- NO hands touching or reaching for food — the couple is focused on each other and the phone
- NO performative gestures (pointing at food, presenting ingredients, looking at camera)
- NO stock-photo energy — this should feel candid, not directed
- NO rushing the emotional beats — let the laughter arrive naturally
- Do not end on a frozen smile at camera — end mid-action, mid-life
